AYGO BABY BUGGY

The urban baby buggy that reinstates freedom of movement for parents in cities. Foldable whenever in just a few seconds. Portable whenever on a daily journey.

By: Mustafa Al Quraishi, Marie Van Der Klink for Imperial College London. With no affiliation to Cilvia Studios.

Problem: Cities are not adapted to allow parents to travel with children around. Obstacles such as going up and down stairs, and mounting public transport is difficult.

Solution: Creating a buggy that can be collapsed and redeployed rapidly on the go.

Design Development

Nowadays, some buggies have been developed to be quick folding, but they all require the baby to be taken out, which makes constant folding throughout a journey inconvenient. Therefore, to allow parents to be effective with their babies in cities with stairs, escalators and in public transports, multiple folding systems were thought of for the urban baby buggy to be foldable wherever, whenever.

Ideation led to an innovative folding process consisting of the separation of the buggy in two parts. This solution would allow parents to transport their buggy easily in two hands without having to take the baby out. This was the first key step towards Aygo.

Prototyping

For the folding process to be effective, the frame has to collapse within seconds and be portable in one hand. Therefore, an innovative folding mechanism was designed.

Solution

Separable in two parts: the baby seat and its frame, Aygo can be folded within seconds. With one hand, the seat is detached from the frame without having to take the baby out. With the other hand, the frame is collapsed and carried around. The innovative folding system enables parents access to previous obstacles in urban areas, such as stairs, and allows flexibility to navigate through crowded spaces. These include transport facilities, such as the underground tube.

How does it work, and is it safe, where can I store essentials?

For the assembly of both parts, it was decided that the carrier would clip onto the frame’s lateral bars. These have a groove that allow the carrier’s internal mechanism to clip onto the frame. The bars are wide and span the entire depth of the frame for increased contact surface area for maximum stability. The underside of the carrier has a handle button located on both ends of the buggy, which makes its access effective for both seat orientations. When pressed, they pull back the clip mechanism and allow the separation of the two parts.

A two step unlock process to collapse the buggy ensures critcal safety, users unlock the handle as signified by the red and green marking. Then compresses the central handle to allow the mechanism to collapse.
