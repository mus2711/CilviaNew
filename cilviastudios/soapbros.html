SOAP BROS

SOAP BROS aims to partner with brands such as Dove and Carex to deliver their products in a more sustainable form, in turn helping them to reach their brand goals. Consumers receive SOAP BROS orbs seamlessly within their shopping order from delivery systems such as Ocado.

By: Mustafa Al Quraishi, Guy Solan, Martin Lombard, Joseph Folkes for Imperial College London. With no affiliation to Cilvia Studios.

Problem: 60% of water use during the handwashing phase is wasted on running water as the user washes their hands. With up to a 1.43L average use when people wash their hands with liquid soap.

Solution: SOAP BROS is here to make hand washing more sustainable. Inspired by molecular gastronomy, a solution of liquid soap and only 5ml of water is suspended in an organic membrane. Removing the waste from packaging and reducing water usage by up to 70%.

Design Development

With our mission to reduce water usage when washing hands, we had to offer a system and service that allowed for seamless integration into peopleâ€™s everyday lives. We carefully developed our brand image and mapped out the user touchpoints.

Prototyping

After experimentation with balls containing 1, 2.5, 5, 10 and 20ml of water - 5ml proved the optimal balance of conservation and efficacy.

Frozen water, soap and calcium lactate are submerged in a sodium alginate solution, as a jelly coating forms. After 5 minutes, cleaning and rinsing removes any water and thus the reaction stops excess jelly formation. The orbs are then ready for use.

Solution

Our solution involved the development of the entire system. We designed the storage containers to hold the soap bros balls which match in size to the industry standard containers. This allows seamless integration into delivery services such as Ocado. We devised the home Soap Bros container that is designed to store the balls for prolonged periods of time, its recycled aluminium construction remains authentic to Soap Brosâ€™ sustainable ethicacy.
